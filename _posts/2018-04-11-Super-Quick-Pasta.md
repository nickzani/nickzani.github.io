In our house we eat a lot of pasta. It is so versatile and works great as a snack with butter and cheese, or an evening meal with a rich meat sauce. It is also a great way of getting kids to eat more vegetables, as you can sneak in onions, courgettes and tomatoes very easily.

If you have never made your own pasta, give it a go. It is great fun and tastes ten times better than anything you can buy from the supermarket. You might not believe me, but give it a go. The difference in taste is incredible. The first time is daunting, but the ingredients are very cheap, and if you mess it up you just end up making playdough instead.

## The Secret Super Simple Recipe

This recipe has two versions. The first takes about 20 minutes, and the second slightly longer. In fact, by the time you have driven to the shops to buy pasta, you could have easily made some yourself. Both are perfect for children to get involved with.

Most pasta recipes are flour and egg, however some Sardinian pastas are actually just flour and water. You have a choice with the flour:

* Type '00' Flour
	- The double zero means the flour is very fine and designed for making pasta
* Semolina
	- This comes from a different type of wheat (durum) and gives a different texture
	
Both types are readily available in most supermarkets. Experiment to find which you prefer. The only piece of equipment you need is a [pasta board](https://www.amazon.co.uk/KitchenCraft-2-Piece-Butter-Gnocchi-Paddles/dp/B019W8N16A/ref=as_li_ss_tl?keywords=pasta+board&qid=1555499229&s=gateway&sr=8-3&linkCode=ll1&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=9dbe49ea84a41d11477133bf5ca3815b&language=en_GB) which can be used for pasta and gnocchi.

## The 20 Minute Version	

1. Weigh out 100g of flour (enough for a couple of people)
2. Measure out 50ml of water
3. Mix the two together roughly with a spoon, and then knead together

![mixed together](/img/posts/pasta3.jpg)

4. Once kneaded, roll out into a long tube, about the same thickness as a pencil

![rolled out](/img/posts/pasta4.jpg)

5. Using a sharp knife slice into small 'pillow' shapes

![pillows](/img/posts/pasta5.jpg)

6. The fun part. Push the pillow onto the pasta board and drag down with your finger, creating a mini spiral. This seems quite time consuming but you will quickly get the hang of it.

![pasta](/img/posts/pasta6.jpg)

7. Cook in boiling water until cooked through. Add butter and parmesan and enjoy. 

![pasta](/img/posts/pasta7.jpg)

## The 1 Hour Version	

Repeat as above but let the dough rest for half an hour in between steps 3 and 4.

## The 1 Day Version	

Repeat as above but let the pasta dry after step 5.

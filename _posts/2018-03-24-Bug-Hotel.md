As spring approaches it is great to get outside more and more. There is less to break in the garden and **much** less tidying to do after a day of running around outside. This activity is a slow burner, which is great because it gives you a reason to keep going outside. It also requires very little supervision, so your children can take it in any direction they like.

## Gather Supplies

The main thing you need for this is a variety of pots or containers. Old plant pots work well, but you can experiment with whatever you like. Old tupperware containers, tins or jars; anything that isn't going to disintergrate when it rains.

Once you have your containers start to hunt around the garden for things to put in them. Most things you can find on the floor will work, so try a variety.

* Pine cones
* Broken up dry sticks
* Moss
* Dried flowers and lavender heads
* Dry leaves
* Chopped up bamboo (this is often what you see used with bee hotels)

A cleaned out plastic bottle with rolled up cardboard inside also works well, with the added bonus that it is easy to see inside.

## Method

Once you have everything, put them into the pots. Dot them around in various places. Try some in the sun and some in the shade.

![Finished](/img/posts/hotel.jpg)

## Revisit After a Week

Check your hotel after a week or two and see if you can spot anything living there.

* Do different materials encourage different insects?
* Does the position in the garden make any difference?

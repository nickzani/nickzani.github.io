Healthy snacks are hard to come by, especially if you have a toddler who needs to eat every hour. There is only so many carrot sticks and apple quarters they will eat before you start looking at other options. This is a great snack for three reasons: it tastes great, involves vegetables and can be made ahead of time and frozen to save time in the future. Most people have something similar to this, but this is what has worked well for me.
  
## Ingredients

For this recipe you only need four ingredients. The amount of each should be varied according to taste and texture:

* One or two broccoli heads
* A few handfuls of grated white cheese (cheddar is the standard but experimentation is encouraged)
* Bread crumbs
* 1 Egg


## Method

This is a simple recipe, and once your children are old enough they can help out too.

1. Put the broccoli heads into a [Nutribullet](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B01N27W15O/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B01N27W15O&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=b1546d1d1491e94197e241efe7b59b96) or similar until it is a crumb or rice consistency
2. Wrap the broccoli in a kitchen towel and squeeze all the water out. Microwave for 5 minutes to soften.
3. Mix in the egg, cheese and bread crumbs. If the mixture is too loose then add more breadcrumbs

![Rolled out](/img/posts/broc_1.jpg)

4. Roll into little cylinders and transfer to a baking tray. Bake at 200C for 25 minutes (or until golden), turning half way

![Baked](/img/posts/broc_2.jpg)


## Freezing

Once you have the bites rolled out, you can freeze in a tupperware box. Take out and bake for instant snacks!

## Edits

This one lends itself nicely to swapping out different ingredients

* Try cauliflower or courgette. Most supermarkets sell packs of cauliflower rice which would make the process even quicker
* Try different cheeses. Mozzarella or parmesan both work well
* As your children get older you can start to include seasoning like salt and pepper

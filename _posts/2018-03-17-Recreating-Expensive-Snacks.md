## Recreating Kiddylicis Apple Wriggles

The price of Kiddylicis Apple Wriggles seems particularly ridiculous. There are only two ingredients!

* Apple Concentrates (Apple Pureé, Apple Juice)
	- Even packs of [prepackaged apple pureé](https://www.amazon.co.uk/GoGo-squeeZ-Apple-Pouches-Multipack/dp/B075BGF47W/ref=as_li_ss_tl?crid=150TBXXVAGS62&keywords=apple+puree&qid=1552474729&s=gateway&sprefix=apple+pur,aps,214&sr=8-1-spons&psc=1&linkCode=ll1&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=cc9b592db1f8258aa84dd5dc67440620&language=en_GB) costs £6.90 a kilo
* Pectin
	- Commonly used as a thickener in jam
	- [Readily available](https://www.amazon.co.uk/Special-Ingredients-Marmalades-Chutneys-Fillings/dp/B00KG6V6H4/ref=as_li_ss_tl?keywords=pectin+sachets&qid=1552475383&s=gateway&sr=8-1&linkCode=ll1&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=94826e4fc013e001dad7c687f6e651a4&language=en_GB) online or in supermarkets 


## Method

This one takes a little bit of trial and error. On the plus side, even if it goes wrong it still tastes delicious, and takes very little time to prepare.

1. Take the apple pureé and put approximately 150g in a small bowl
2. Add in around 8g of pectin
3. Stir this together. You will notice the apple will instantly become very gloopy and hard to stir
4. Spoon this out onto a sheet of baking parchment and spread relatively thinly. This is the part that takes some trial and error. Try and get the spread as even as possible to stop it cooking unevenly
5. Place in an oven and cook over a low heat for 30 mins (approximately 100 degrees C). Peel off the baking parchment, flip over and do the same on the other side.
6. Place onto a chopping board and slice thinly. If they are slightly sticky still then you can dust with corn flour

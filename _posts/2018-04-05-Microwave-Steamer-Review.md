I love using the microwave. The cooking time is very quick, and there is a minimal amount of waiting time. No need for an oven to heat up or a kettle to boil. There is also the massive benefit of no pots and pans to clean up at the end. I love my microwave so much that I can easily whip up a week's worth of meals in 10 minutes.

## My Foolproof Iceland Meal

Iceland gets a bad rap. Yes, they have a whole section stocking nothing but Gregg's food, and yes there are a lot of frozen chips, but if you haven't been in a while go and have a look. Their Vegan section is great, and their 'No Bull' burgers in particular are a great alternative to meat, and their commitment to ditch plastic and palm oil is something all supermarkets should be following.

Frozen fish, frozen hours after being caught at sea, can often be fresher than unfrozen, which has taken a few days to reach the fish counter. Because of this I like to go down occasionally and stock up on bags of fish and veg. For this meal you only need three ingredients

* A bag of frozen fish
	- Sea bass, cod or salmon are all good choices
* A bag of frozen mixed vegetables
	- If you can't find them mixed then buy them separately and mix them together or alternatively Waitrose do a bag of mixed mediterranean vegetables
* A baking potato or sweet potato

## Method

1. Microwave the fish for the recommended amount of time on the packet
2. Microwave the veg until it is cooked
3. Microwave the potato or sweet potato until it is soft in the middle
4. Mix together

When mixing add some water or low salt stock or alternatively butter to loosen if needed.

## Microwave Steamer

A while ago I bought a [microwave steamer](https://www.amazon.co.uk/Sistema-Microwave-Medium-Steamer-Removable/dp/B005D6XYGS/ref=as_li_ss_tl?keywords=microwave+steamer&qid=1554977830&s=gateway&sr=8-1-spons&psc=1&linkCode=ll1&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=472ccb0b5f7df6c20850c57cc5619049&language=en_GB) with the aim of speeding up these quick meals.

![Microwave Steamer](/img/posts/steam2.jpg)

I wanted to love it, and I used it a few times. The process is simple - add the vegetables into the basket, put the water in the box and then put in the microwave. 

In the end I decided it didn't really add anything over a bowl with some water tipped in and some clingfilm over the top. It was also bulky and took up a lot of space in the dishwasher, so for that reason I didn't carry on using it, but I think it would be good if you were cooking bulky things in a microwave. A lot of broccoli chopped up for example, which might not fit into a bowl.

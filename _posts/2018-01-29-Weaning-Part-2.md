By now you are probably a few months into weaning, and are a dab hand at blending various fruits and vegetables together. The next stage is the introduction of meat, and some more textured meals. In some ways this can seem more daunting, but it is actually a great step on the road to them being able to eat what you eat.

Of course, there are a [wide range of stage 2 weaning pouches available](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B006CQR6OE/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&tag=howtodad-21&camp=1634&creative=6738&linkCode=as2&creativeASIN=B006CQR6OE&linkId=af7ad62d8df9a7f06d30377c9c19784b) but you will find the cost of these adds up quickly. Also, try these yourselves and then compare to some of the home cooked recipes you can make below and see which you prefer...

## Make Your Favourite Meals but Without the Salt

Continue to [puree your child's food](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B01N27W15O/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B01N27W15O&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=b1546d1d1491e94197e241efe7b59b96) but start to incorporate your favourite meals. Leave the salt out, and then add it in later when you eat. [These low salt stock cubes](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B00DJTCX9K/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B00DJTCX9K&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=80a731c685280ab3af977bc88d6c4808) were a game changer, since a lot of recipes contain stock. The other alternative is to buy some chicken and vegetables and boil some up yourself. [Jamie Oliver](https://www.jamieoliver.com/recipes/chicken-recipes/easy-chicken-stock/) has a great recipe. Make a big batch and freeze it.

## Embrace the Slow Cooker

A [slow cooker](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B002NPC0RG/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B002NPC0RG&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=54eacf4286394f2cb0c52fde90064e86) is a great addition to any kitchen, regardless of whether you have a baby or not. There are [loads of great articles](http://snigskitchen.blogspot.com/2013/01/slow-cooker-101-aka-crock-pot-101-or.html) on how to choose one, and you will find they vary in size, colour and materials. [Get yourself any decent slow cooker cookbook](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/1905862660/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&tag=howtodad-21&camp=1634&creative=6738&linkCode=as2&creativeASIN=1905862660&linkId=26d7b27d585068377ba7b79e7e51867d) and leave out the salt in recipes, and swap out stock for the low salt variety.

## A Very Quick Baby Friendly Meal

This meal, and varients of it have been a life saver during a busy week when both parents are working:

* A cheap cut of meat. Stewing beef, shin or skirt work well. Ox cheek is also great if you can get down to a butchers or your supermarket has a meat counter
* A pack of frozen mixed vegetables. Waitrose do a great frozen soffritto mix. Keep it in the freezer so you can chuck it in whenever needed
* [Low salt stock cubes](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B00DJTCX9K/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B00DJTCX9K&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=80a731c685280ab3af977bc88d6c4808)
* Tin of tomatoes or a jar of passata
* Add everything into the slow cooker and leave it to cook on low throughout the day. Just before needed [blend](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B01N27W15O/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B01N27W15O&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=b1546d1d1491e94197e241efe7b59b96), adding some water if it becomes too thick
* For your own dinner transfer to a pot on the hob and boil off any excess liquid, add salt and pepper as needed

## Other Quick Cook Alternatives

* The [Little Pasta Sauces](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B00AQGGMXW/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&tag=howtodad-21&camp=1634&creative=6738&linkCode=as2&creativeASIN=B00AQGGMXW&linkId=297e8357a320b3eeb1a857c5194a2322) are super quick to heat up and add to pasta
* Some of the [Tilda Rice Pouches](https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/B0048EZVGW/ref=as_li_ss_tl?psc=1&pd_rd_i=B0048EZVGW&linkCode=ll1&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=5dea7b2b3cc189b8e4a92a807d64906f&language=en_GB) are salt free and can be heated up in the microwave. Great combined with a stew. Just check the ingredients to make sure they do not contain any added salt.
* Jacket potatoes can be cooked in 5 minutes in the microwave, and can then be combined with tuna, mayonnaise and sweetcorn for a quick and healthy lunch

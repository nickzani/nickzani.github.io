Baby bath time is a traditional dad activity. You will come back from the hospital, and after a few days of changing nappies be excited to be finally able to take on a real piece of baby responsibility. This is probably how you imagine it will go:

![Happy baby bath](/img/posts/happybath.jpg)

Sorry to break it to you, but it more than likely will be like this:

![Sad baby bath](/img/posts/sadbath.jpg)

My own experience of bathing a baby varied from relaxing and fun, to shouting and crying. The graph below sums up how quickly this changed; literally day to day!

![Graphing Bath Enjoyment](/img/posts/bathenjoymentjpg.jpg)

Luckily you will see the light at the end of the tunnel: the plateau of enjoyment, where every bath was enjoyable.

![Graphing Bath Enjoyment](/img/posts/bathenjoymentjpgcircle.jpg)

Let's start with the day you bring your baby home from the hospital.

## Top and Tailing
A few days after you bring your baby home, you may hear references to 'top and tailing' your baby by the health visitor or midwife if they visit. Let me break the process for this down:
* Get a sponge and wipe your baby's head
* Wipe your baby's bottom

I never worked out exactly why this process needed it's own name, and I think calling it 'clean your baby without touching the manky umbilical cord' is a better name. Or even better still 'wipe your baby'. You can use a bowl from the kitchen and doft sponge for this, or buy [something specifically designed for the purpose](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B001383X4S/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B001383X4S&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=af697d34ce940987ed3ffb325cc9065a), with a bowl for the top and one for the tail. Once the umbilical cord falls off, proper bath time can begin.

## What Makes no Difference: Calming Bubble Bath or Body Washes
Buy them because they smell nice, or work well with your babies skin, but I found they made no difference to how my baby slept. I find the [Childs Farm](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B01FFXJRG4/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B01FFXJRG4&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=1ecbde7bc3bb38487c2ac721091d5c89) ones are nice.

## Invest in a Baby Seat
Initially bath time went very badly for me, until I realised my baby hated (and still does!) lying down in the bath looking up. One of the best ever purchases I made was a [bath seat](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B00AWMV9CY/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B00AWMV9CY&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=73a39d500814ec27f7038d5b4e4b9ea5) that they could sit in.

## Buy a Bigger Seat When They are a Toddler
After a while they will grow out of the baby seat, but be in an awkward period where they can't properly sit up on their own. A [bigger bath seat](https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B00CMR3H04/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&camp=1634&creative=6738&creativeASIN=B00CMR3H04&linkCode=as2&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=3033657c0fd0164535c026bb0e93aab8) like this one really helped for our toddler. It is able to swivel round as well which adds to the fun. Eventually they will grow out of this and be able to sit unaided in the bath.

There is a lot of energy in wax. When you burn wax it gives out 42 kJ of energy per gram. That is 42,000 Joules of energy. For comparison, a Snickers bar contains 20 kJ per gram and butter is about 30. What is amazing is that this energy can be used to cook. This seems counter-intuitive as when we usually interact with candles they are on a birthday cake or providing light, and seem to give off very little heat. The reason for this is that heat rises, and is quickly lost in a large room. When contained, it quickly builds up.

## You Will Need

* A tin can, drained and opened at one end
* [Tea lights](https://www.amazon.co.uk/100-candles-tealights-IKEA/dp/B0196JXOLW/ref=as_li_ss_tl?keywords=tea+lights&qid=1553505207&s=gateway&sr=8-7&linkCode=ll1&tag=howtodad-21&linkId=24ed6f44b5f64e39d6d17831de6c00cb&language=en_GB), available everywhere and are very cheap
* Eggs
* Flour
* Milk
* Butter or oil to cook with

## Method

Take the tin can and give it a good clean. Drill some holes in it at different points to let the air in (don't drill too many or the heat will escape). I put the can and candles on a piece of wood to keep the table below safe.

![The cooker](/img/posts/pancake1.jpg)

Mix up the pancake batter according to your usual recipe. I used the [BBC one](https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/recipes/2907669/easy-pancakes) which makes crepe style pancakes. 

I found that the number of tea lights used was key. Too few and the pancake cooks too slowly and sticks to the can, so try and use at least two. Light them and then put the can on the top. Put some butter or oil on the top of the can and wait for it to get hot; it will happen surprisingly quickly. Be careful at this point not to burn yourself. Pour some pancake mix on to the top of the can:

![Pancake](/img/posts/pancake2.jpg)

Once you see bubbles start to appear you know it is working. Take a spatula and try and flip it. In total it will take a few minutes per pancake. Put some sugar and lemon juice on and enjoy!
